This project deals with a public health topic that interests me a lot, as my works focus also on health determinants, such as the body size determinant.
Here, I worked with the dataset from the ESCAPAD survey (Enquête sur la santé et les consommations lors de la journée défense et citoyenneté) led by the French Observatory on Drugs and Addictive Behaviours. 
In this survey, scientists collected data since 2000 during the Defence and Citizenship Day (JDC), where they asked 17-years-old French boys and girls about their general health, as well as their use of psychoactive substances and addictive behaviours.

Focusing on body size, two topics emerged from this dataset: obesity and thinness. These body size categories are based on the World Health Organisation's recommendations for body mass index, where obesity corresponds to a BMI > 30 and thinness to a BMI > 18.5.
With this dataset, we can analyze French teenagers' body size over time and across geographical locations (=regions). 

In this repository, each folder contains files for analysing obesity and thinness respectively: Excel files for boys and girls, a scientific poster and infographic, and the script coded in R.
